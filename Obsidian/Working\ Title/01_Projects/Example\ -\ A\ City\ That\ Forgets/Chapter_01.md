# Chapter 01: The Note

Maya's handwriting was the only thing she trusted. Every morning, the same ritual: rise before the others, find yesterday's note, read it aloud to lock the words in place.

*I am Maya. I am a nurse at Riverside Hospital. My daughter Zara is fourteen. We live in the south wing, apartment 417. The man in 418 is not my husband.*

She'd written that last part three days running before the details stuck. Not my husband. Never had been. But every morning she'd wake wondering who he was, why his coffee was in the kitchen, why she felt his absence in her bones.

The morning book was hidden behind the loose panel in her bathroom. Blue cover, her handwriting crowded on every page. She ran her thumb over Tuesday's entry.

*Dr. Reeves called. He knows I've been forgetting. He's offering the trial. Don't say yes. Don't say yes.*

Her hands shook. A trial for what? She read further, squinting at the small, frantic letters.

*If you remember this: you've been forgetting more. Zara left a note on the kitchen table. Find it before she wakes up. She's scared.*

Maya closed the book. The apartment was gray with pre-dawn light. She moved through the hallway, careful not to wake Zara, and found the note weighted down by Zara's phone on the kitchen table.

The handwriting was Zara's—all loops and haste.

*Mom, I'm scared. You forgot my name yesterday. You kept asking who the girl was. I told you I was a client, just to make it stop. You believed me. Mom, there's something wrong. Please go to the doctor. I can't—*

The rest was scratched out so hard the pen had torn the paper.

Maya sat at the table in the dark kitchen and waited for her daughter to wake up, holding two pieces of paper, neither of them her own memory.
