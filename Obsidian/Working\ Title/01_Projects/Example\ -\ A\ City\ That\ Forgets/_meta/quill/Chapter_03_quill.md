## Major Suggestions

### ¶1 — Passive phrasing in key moment

**Original:** "Three weeks into the trial, Maya woke without reaching for her note. This alone was new. The reaching had been automatic as breathing."

**Issue:** The reach-for-the-note is the most important gesture in the whole story, and you're backing into it with passives. "The reaching had been automatic" tells rather than shows. The power is in the *action* of reaching, not in narrating its automaticity.

**Suggestion:** Start with the absence of the gesture. Something like: *"Three weeks into the trial, Maya woke—and did not reach for her note. No automatic reach. No hand to the loose panel."* You're showing the negation, which is stronger than stating it. This moment anchors the whole chapter's hope.

**Note:** This pairs with ¶9, where her trust in the note-less morning wavers. The two moments should feel like mirror actions. Right now the opening is too abstract.

### ¶7 — Show her fear of forgetting through behavior, not summary

**Original:** "This was foolish, maybe. Overconfident. But she wanted to know if the compound was working or if she was just having a good morning."

**Issue:** You're summarizing her internal deliberation. "This was foolish" is you editorializing. Instead, show her hesitation through action. The coffee-making *instead* of checking the book—that's the show. Lean on that. Cut the commentary.

**Suggestion:** End the previous paragraph with her making coffee and taking it to the window (or wherever). Let the reader infer her hope and fear from the *choice* not to check the book. The action carries the whole weight.

### ¶8 — "Fractured" is doing a lot of work; be more specific

**Original:** "When Maya came in, Zara looked up, and something fractured in her face."

**Issue:** "Fractured" is vivid, but in a vague way. Faces don't fracture—expressions shift, collapse, harden, crack. What did Zara's face *do*? The specificity will let us see her fear or joy more clearly. Right now it's pretty but impressionistic.

**Suggestion:** Is it surprise? Relief? Terror? Pick the one that matters, then find the physical detail that shows it. *"Zara looked up, and her eyes widened—then narrowed, as if she was bracing for a trick."* That's specificity. It also hints at her fear that Maya's "change" is temporary or dangerous.

## Voice Notes

### Prose register shift in ¶10

**Note:** Maya's voice in ¶10 ("Zarah. ... Just Zara.") is clipped and confident, but three paragraphs later (¶13) she's apologetic and hedging ("Ask me tomorrow. ... I always do. That part doesn't change."). This shift is *intentional* and *good*—it shows her encountering Zara's fear and pulling back. But watch that it doesn't read as uncertainty in your authorial voice. The reader should feel her actively *choosing* softness, not fumbling for words. The line "Ask me tomorrow" helps sell that choice, but the lines after it dilute it.

**Reframe:** End that exchange with "Ask me tomorrow," then let the coffee-drinking be its own paragraph. Then the note-writing. Let her softness be a *decision* followed by action, not a kind of apologetic backpedaling.
