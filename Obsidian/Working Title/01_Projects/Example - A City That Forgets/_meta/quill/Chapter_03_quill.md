## Major Suggestions

### The key gesture is narrated, not shown
- **Location:** "Three weeks into the trial, Maya woke—" (¶1–2)
- **Issue:** Not reaching for the note is the most important gesture in the story, and the prose backs into it — "The reaching had been automatic as breathing" tells the reader about the habit instead of letting its absence land.
- **Suggestion:** Let the negation carry the sentence. As written: "Maya woke without reaching for her note. This alone was new." Tighter: "Maya woke — and did not reach for her note." The explanation of automaticity can be cut; the reader has watched the ritual for two chapters.

### Editorializing dilutes the coffee choice
- **Location:** "She made coffee instead of checking the—" (¶4)
- **Issue:** "This was foolish, maybe. Overconfident." summarizes the deliberation the action already dramatizes. The coffee-instead-of-the-book *is* the show; the commentary is the tell.
- **Suggestion:** Cut "This was foolish, maybe. Overconfident." and let the paragraph move directly from the choice to "But she wanted to know…" — the risk reads clearly without the label.

### "Fractured" is vivid but vague
- **Location:** "Zara was already awake, at the kitchen—" (¶5)
- **Issue:** "Something fractured in her face" gestures at an emotion without committing to one. Faces don't fracture; expressions shift, collapse, harden. The reader can't tell if Zara feels hope, fear, or both — and this is the beat where it matters most.
- **Suggestion:** Replace "fractured" with the specific movement that carries the intended emotion — as written: "something fractured in her face"; alternatives depending on intent: "her face went very still" / "her expression cracked open". Pick the reading you mean.

## Voice Notes

### Maya — ¶7–12
- **As written:** "Zarah." … "I don't know. Ask me tomorrow." … "I'll leave you a note. I always do. That part doesn't change."
- **Atlas voice / speech markers:** Precise, formal when stressed; hedges spoken commitments; repeats key phrases.
- **Suggestion:** The shift from the clipped "Zarah" exchange to the hedged, softening lines is consistent with her profile and clearly intentional — flagging only to confirm the softness reads as a *choice* Maya makes when she sees Zara's fear. "Ask me tomorrow" sells the choice; the two sentences after it slightly dilute it and could end one beat earlier.
