## Reader Confusion Points

### Who is "the man," again?
- **Location:** "This alone was new. The reaching had—" (¶2)
- **Issue:** "The man who'd been not-her-husband for two years" asks me to connect this figure to Ch.1's unnamed cohabitant and to hold "two years" as new information about him at the same time. I made the connection, but it took a beat — the phrasing is efficient but cold, and this is the first time his duration in her life has a number.
- **Severity:** Minor
- **Suggestion:** An echo of Ch.1's language about him (waking beside someone without remembering choosing them) would anchor the reference — the connective tissue is what's missing, not more information.

### What is Zara feeling here?
- **Location:** "Zara was already awake, at the kitchen—" (¶5)
- **Issue:** "Something fractured in her face" left me suspended between readings — joy? terror? The next line ("You remembered my name") resolves *what happened* but not *how she feels about it*. If the ambiguity is deliberate — I'm seeing Zara through Maya's still-recovering eyes — it mostly works, but I noticed myself stopping to decide.
- **Severity:** Minor
- **Suggestion:** One physical detail tipping the emotional register would settle it; or keep the ambiguity, knowing the reader pauses here.

## Emotional Notes

### "That's the part that scares me"
- **Location:** "Zara reached across the table and took—" (¶13)
- **Observation:** This is the emotional climax of the whole story for me. Three chapters of rooting for Maya's recovery, and Zara turns it over in one line: the return is possible, but the person returning is someone new. It reframes everything I'd been hoping for.

### The last line reaches — and just about earns it
- **Location:** "She left the book where her daughter—" (¶18)
- **Observation:** "The closest thing to faith" is a big note to end on. After the blue book's role in all three chapters, it lands — but only just. The image of the book left where Zara can find it is already carrying the meaning; the abstraction rides on top of it.

## What Works
_Moments that land well. Useful to know what not to change._

- The opening — waking without reaching for the note — pays off two chapters of ritual in one absent gesture. I felt the change before it was explained.
- The "Zarah / Just Zara" correction does enormous work in four words: recovery is real, and imperfect, and Zara has been braced for both.
- Writing in the blue book at the end reads differently than in Ch.1 — not survival anymore, but witness. Same act, transformed meaning, no narration needed.

## Overall impression
The chapter closes the story where it should: recovery arrives, and it costs. I finished holding two feelings at once — relief that Maya came back, and Zara's fear that someone else did. The unresolved ending feels right for this world; a city that forgets can't promise permanence, and the story doesn't pretend otherwise.
